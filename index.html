Coq/SSReflect Algebraic Geometry

Algebraic Geometry in Coq

Welcome to the homepage of the Coq algebraic geometry project. Small algebraic geometric developments have been done in Coq many times, but as far as we know there has been no bottom-up development of modern (Grothendieck-style) algebraic geometry in Coq. This project is intended to fill this gap, and also to demonstrate that Coq and SSReflect/MathComp is capable of handling high-level, abstract mathematics, in addition to low-level, concrete mathematics which it is well-known to be able to handle well.

This project is in many ways inspired by the Stacks Project, in that we also start with few preliminaries and build up the tools necessary to develop algebraic geometry step-by-step; it is also motivated in part by Lean's Mathlib. We take as granted Coq's expressive Gallina specification language, the SSReflect proof language and the Coq Mathematical Components library, and formalize the required stepping stones towards algebraic geometry. We do not necessarily follow the Stacks Project in our formalization, but like the Stacks Project, we aim to build a coherent library of related mathematics along our way. Eventually, however, this project would not necessarily be limited to algebraic geometry: we are also interested in expanding in other related directions, such as homological algebra and topos theory.

Current & planned developments

Currently, our main goal is to define the notion of an affine scheme, i.e. a locally ringed space isomorphic to the spectrum of a commutative ring with unit. This requires a bit of category theory and commutative algebra, as well as some sheaf theory.

  • Category theory
    (main reference: Emily Riehl, Category Theory in Context)
  • Commutative algebra
    (main reference: David Eisenbud, Commutative Algebra with A View Towards Algebraic Geometry, GTM 150)

  • Basic scheme theory
    (main reference: David Eisenbud and Joe Harris, The Geometry of Schemes, GTM 197)

Besides the main reference text listed under each item, we also refer frequently to the Stacks Project, and to Robin Hartshorne's Algebraic Geometry.

Note

We intend to do a formalization of "textbook" algebraic geometry, which relies heavily on non-constructive reasoning principles (especially the Axiom of Choice). Therefore, we always assume functional and proof extensionality, decidable equality for every type, and the type-theoretic Axiom of Choice in our development.
